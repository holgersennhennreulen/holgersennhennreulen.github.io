home / draw observations from published forest biometrics models

(statistical) models serve several purposes. according to korner-nievergelt et al. (2015, p. 1), they:

  • "1) models help to describe how we think a system works,
  • (2) data can be summarized using models,
  • (3) comparison of model predictions with data helps with understanding the system, and
  • (4) models allow for predictions, including the quantification of their uncertainty, and, therefore, they help with making decisions."

i am interested in statistical probabilstic models that can be written down in the mathematical form of a probability (density/mass) function, which is "an object with two methods. the first method being a probability density (or distribution) function that computes [the value of the function] [\ldots], and the second a method that draws exact samples from said distribution." (van de meent et al, 2018, p. 14)

this webpage is dedicated to applying this second method to published models in forest biometrics. i am motivated to do this exercise as i hope it:<\h3>
